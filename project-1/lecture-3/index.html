    Lecture 3

    Where is the Internet (network of networks)

    A common representation is to think of the Internet as a cloud. Millions of copper
    and fiber optic cables connecting millions of server computers and other
    networked devices!

    From the Computer to Outside the Home

    The broadband modem links your traffic to an outside distribution hub. It
    does not route any traffic. It's more of a gate between digital traffic and
    physical medium (fiber/coax/DSL). The hub (head end) is usually provided by the
    internet service provider (ISP), and links up multiple houses/buildings to
    the hub (head end).

    The wireless router forwards data packets from one network to another (like a
    normal router), except it receives packets wirelessly

    From that head end, higher bandwidth connections lead to a central ISP
    which sends that traffic to an Internet Exchange Point (ISP Point). Data Centers
    are usually directly connected here, as they require very high bandwidth. Then
    from the ISP point, it goes to the Landing Station which connnects to wires
    under the ocean.
